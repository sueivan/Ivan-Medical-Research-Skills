# Medical Communication Harm Audit

## Misinterpretation

- Could readers mistake association for causation?
- Could preclinical evidence be mistaken for human benefit?
- Could a research biomarker be mistaken for an available clinical test?
- Could a surrogate outcome be mistaken for improved health or survival?
- Could relative risk create fear or false reassurance?

## Behavior

- Could readers self-diagnose?
- Could readers stop or change proven treatment?
- Could readers purchase an unproven or harmful product?
- Could the piece delay professional or emergency care?

## Equity and dignity

- Does wording blame patients or caregivers?
- Does it stigmatize age, disability, mental illness, body size, genetics, or culture?
- Are access, cost, and population applicability considered?

## Presentation

- Does the headline exceed the body evidence?
- Do images imply precision, causality, or availability not supported by evidence?
- Are conflicts, limitations, harms, and uncertainty visible enough?
