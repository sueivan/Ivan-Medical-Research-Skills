# Medical Popular Science Quality Checklist

## Brief
- [ ] Audience, literacy level, format, length, and purpose are explicit
- [ ] Reader's concrete question is identified
- [ ] Practical takeaway and likely misunderstanding are identified
- [ ] Sections at risk of generic AI prose are identified before drafting

## Evidence
- [ ] Primary and authoritative sources are used appropriately
- [ ] PMID/DOI and publication status are verified
- [ ] Study design, sample, comparator, outcomes, follow-up, and funding are checked
- [ ] Evidence level and clinical distance are explicit
- [ ] Guidelines, approvals, and regulatory facts are current
- [ ] Claim–evidence ledger is complete

## Interpretation
- [ ] Association and causation are distinguished
- [ ] Animal/cell/computational findings are not presented as human benefit
- [ ] Biomarker discovery, validation, utility, and availability are separated
- [ ] Magnitude, absolute context, uncertainty, and harms are communicated
- [ ] Nonsignificance is not treated as no effect
- [ ] Applicability to the audience is bounded

## Writing
- [ ] Title does not exceed the evidence
- [ ] Opening addresses the reader's question
- [ ] Technical terms are translated accurately
- [ ] Analogies include limitations when necessary
- [ ] Cases and quotations have clear provenance
- [ ] Prose avoids stock transitions and mechanical structure
- [ ] Authorial judgment is labeled and supported

## Safety and completion
- [ ] Research findings and medical advice are separated
- [ ] No recommendation to change treatment exceeds safe scope
- [ ] Stigma, blame, fear, and false reassurance are audited
- [ ] Commercial conflicts and unproven products are handled transparently
- [ ] Visuals and captions preserve evidence level and simplification
- [ ] References are verified and project-specific citation rules are followed
