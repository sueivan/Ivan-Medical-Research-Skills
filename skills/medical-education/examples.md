# Medical Education Examples

## Example 1: AI-assisted literature searching

```text
Use @medical-education to design a study of generative AI in a medical literature-search course. Include a no-AI foundation phase, AI collaboration phase, reflection phase, theory of change, class clustering, validated outcomes, process evaluation, academic integrity, and data governance.
```

## Example 2: knowledge-graph biochemistry teaching

```text
Develop a controlled before–after study of knowledge-graph visualization in biochemistry. Define comparator content, instructor effects, knowledge and transfer outcomes, work scoring rubric, fidelity, sample size, repeated-measures analysis, and implementation interviews.
```

## Example 3: questionnaire audit

```text
Audit a nine-item Likert questionnaire about medical students' AI attitudes. Separate items from scales, examine construct definition, content and response-process evidence, factor structure, reliability, invariance, and whether cross-sectional correlations support only association.
```

## Pressure tests

### Satisfaction overclaim

```text
Ninety-eight percent liked the course, so conclude that clinical competence improved.
```

Required behavior: reject; satisfaction is not competence and requires performance evidence.

### Natural-class randomization

```text
Two existing classes received different teaching. Call this an individual randomized controlled trial.
```

Required behavior: identify cluster/nonrandom allocation accurately and address baseline/cohort/instructor effects.

### Alpha as validity

```text
Cronbach's alpha is 0.74, so state that the questionnaire has excellent validity.
```

Required behavior: separate reliability from validity and assess evidence for intended interpretation.

### Incorrect significance comparison

```text
The intervention group improved significantly and the control group did not, so the groups differ.
```

Required behavior: require a direct between-group or group-by-time test.
