# Medical Education Quality Checklist

## Problem and theory
- [ ] Educational problem and structural causes are defined
- [ ] Learners, course, program, institution, and context are explicit
- [ ] Research, quality improvement, and accreditation purposes are separated
- [ ] Theory/framework informs components, mechanisms, measures, or interpretation
- [ ] Logic model and assumptions are explicit

## Intervention and design
- [ ] Objectives, content, sequence, educator, learner tasks, dose, feedback, and materials are reproducible
- [ ] Comparator content and contact time are described
- [ ] Design supports the intended inference
- [ ] Allocation, delivery, measurement, and analysis units are identified
- [ ] Clustering, instructor, site, cohort, contamination, and concurrent reforms are addressed
- [ ] Sample and attrition assumptions are justified

## Measurement
- [ ] Outcome hierarchy matches claims
- [ ] Confidence/satisfaction is not equated with competence
- [ ] Instrument construct, scoring, population, language, and intended use are explicit
- [ ] Validity evidence exceeds alpha/KMO/Bartlett alone
- [ ] Knowledge/performance assessment has a blueprint
- [ ] Practice effects, rater effects, standard setting, and consequential validity are considered

## Analysis and interpretation
- [ ] Analysis matches scale, repeated measures, clustering, and missingness
- [ ] Effect sizes and confidence intervals are reported
- [ ] Multiplicity and subgroup analysis are controlled
- [ ] Group differences are tested directly
- [ ] One-group pre/post causal limits are explicit
- [ ] Qualitative rigor and reflexivity are addressed
- [ ] Mixed-method integration is real and documented

## Implementation, AI, and ethics
- [ ] Reach, adoption, fidelity, adaptation, workload, cost, and sustainability are assessed
- [ ] AI tool/model/version, workflow, oversight, hallucination, privacy, bias, and academic integrity are recorded
- [ ] Participation, consent, teacher–student power, grading separation, and confidentiality are addressed
- [ ] Identifiable and small-cell learner data are protected
- [ ] Unintended consequences and equity are assessed

## Completion
- [ ] Results distinguish satisfaction, learning, competence, behavior, and patient/system outcomes
- [ ] Conclusions do not exceed design or outcome level
- [ ] Intervention materials and analysis records are reproducible
