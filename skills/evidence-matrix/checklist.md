# Evidence Matrix Quality Checklist

## Design
- [ ] Matrix purpose and downstream use are stated
- [ ] Unit of analysis is defined
- [ ] Eligibility criteria and study designs are stated
- [ ] Data dictionary is available
- [ ] Form was piloted when feasible

## Identity and provenance
- [ ] Every study has a stable `study_id`
- [ ] Every source has a `report_id`
- [ ] Multiple reports are linked
- [ ] PMID/DOI identity is verified or qualified
- [ ] Source locations are recorded when feasible
- [ ] Values are labeled reported, calculated, inferred, unclear, or not reported

## Extraction
- [ ] Design and setting are captured
- [ ] Population and diagnostic criteria are captured
- [ ] Intervention/exposure and comparator definitions are sufficient
- [ ] Outcomes, instruments, units, and time points are explicit
- [ ] Analysis populations and denominators are explicit
- [ ] Adjusted and unadjusted estimates are separated
- [ ] Effect measures and uncertainty are preserved
- [ ] Funding and conflicts are captured when relevant

## Quality control
- [ ] Arm totals and denominators are checked
- [ ] Units and effect direction are checked
- [ ] Tables, figures, supplements, registries, and companion reports are reconciled
- [ ] Overlapping cohorts are assessed
- [ ] Missing values are not treated as zero
- [ ] Conflicts are logged rather than hidden

## Integrity and completion
- [ ] No data are invented
- [ ] Abstract-only extraction is labeled
- [ ] Author limitations and reviewer judgments are separated
- [ ] Formal bias judgments use a named appropriate tool
- [ ] Another reviewer could reconstruct the extraction
