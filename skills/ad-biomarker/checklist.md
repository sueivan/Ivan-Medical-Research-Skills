# Alzheimer Disease Biomarker Quality Checklist

## Intended use and population
- [ ] Intended use and decision consequence are explicit
- [ ] AD definition and disease stage are stated
- [ ] Population, setting, referral pathway, and prevalence are stated
- [ ] Comparator and reference standard match the use case
- [ ] Comorbidities and spectrum effects are considered

## Measurement
- [ ] Biomarker domain, modality, specimen, analyte/feature, platform, and version are recorded
- [ ] Preanalytical procedures are described
- [ ] Analytical sensitivity, precision, reproducibility, interference, range, and QC are assessed
- [ ] Imaging/digital/omics preprocessing and versioning are reproducible
- [ ] Cross-platform interchangeability is not assumed

## Validation
- [ ] Study design matches intended use
- [ ] Threshold derivation is transparent
- [ ] Same-dataset optimization is not called external validation
- [ ] Sensitivity/specificity or prediction measures include uncertainty
- [ ] Predictive values are interpreted with prevalence
- [ ] Calibration is assessed when applicable
- [ ] Independent external validation is assessed
- [ ] Incremental value uses a relevant clinical baseline

## Utility and implementation
- [ ] Accuracy and clinical utility are separated
- [ ] Patient-relevant benefits and harms are considered
- [ ] False-positive, false-negative, and indeterminate consequences are considered
- [ ] Surrogate claims are justified or rejected
- [ ] Regulation, guideline, reimbursement, and availability are distinguished
- [ ] Current status is verified by jurisdiction/date
- [ ] Cost, infrastructure, ethics, counseling, and equity are assessed

## Completion
- [ ] Biomarker positivity is not equated with dementia
- [ ] Biomarker change is not equated with disease modification
- [ ] Genetic risk is not presented as destiny
- [ ] Research-only status is explicit
- [ ] Personal testing/treatment advice is excluded
