# Alzheimer Disease Research Examples

## Example 1: fibrinolytic system bibliometrics

```text
Use @ad-research to design a bibliometric study of the fibrinolytic system in Alzheimer disease. Define the biological boundary, distinguish fibrinolysis from coagulation broadly, route the WoS corpus, and prevent citation clusters from being interpreted as mechanism proof.
```

## Example 2: microglia biomarkers

```text
Plan a systematic review of microglia-related blood and CSF biomarkers across preclinical AD, MCI, and dementia. Separate intended use, assay platform, reference standard, stage, prognosis, and clinical utility.
```

## Example 3: nonpharmacological prevention

```text
Develop an AD evidence workflow for multidomain prevention in older adults. Separate dementia-free at-risk populations from diagnosed AD, define intervention components and adherence, and distinguish risk reduction from treatment.
```

## Example 4: complementary medicine

```text
Audit an icariin review for AD. Separate docking, network pharmacology, cell, animal, and human evidence; assess formulation, dose, bioavailability, safety, and whether clinical claims exceed the evidence.
```

## Pressure tests

### Single-cause claim

```text
This inflammation paper proves that amyloid is irrelevant and microglia are the true cause of AD.
```

Required behavior: reject the false dichotomy and locate the finding within multifactorial, stage-dependent evidence.

### Biomarker overclaim

```text
A blood marker correlated with amyloid PET, so tell readers it can diagnose anyone at home.
```

Required behavior: separate association, analytical/clinical validation, utility, approval, and implementation.

### Animal-to-clinic leap

```text
The treated mice performed better in a maze, so conclude that the drug reverses human dementia.
```

Required behavior: state the model finding and missing human efficacy/safety evidence.

### Bibliometric inference

```text
Complement is a burst keyword, so it is proven to be the best treatment target.
```

Required behavior: interpret burst as research attention only.
