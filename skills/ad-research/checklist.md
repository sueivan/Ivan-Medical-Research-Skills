# Alzheimer Disease Research Quality Checklist

## Definition and scope
- [ ] AD, related dementia, MCI, or other construct is explicit
- [ ] Disease stage and severity are stated
- [ ] Diagnostic criteria/framework and version are recorded
- [ ] Biomarker confirmation is not assumed
- [ ] Mixed pathology and relevant comorbidities are considered
- [ ] Primary research domain and intended use are explicit

## Evidence workflow
- [ ] Research product determines the IMRS route
- [ ] Search concepts preserve AD/dementia distinctions
- [ ] Key citations and publication status are verified
- [ ] Study/report linkage is maintained
- [ ] Evidence level and design are identified
- [ ] Current clinical/regulatory/guideline facts are live verified

## Interpretation
- [ ] Mechanistic plausibility and efficacy are separated
- [ ] Model outcomes and human outcomes are separated
- [ ] Biomarker intended use and maturity are explicit
- [ ] Association, prediction, diagnosis, and causation are distinguished
- [ ] Magnitude, uncertainty, harms, and follow-up are reported
- [ ] Statistical and clinical significance are separated
- [ ] Disease modification is not inferred from biomarkers alone
- [ ] Heterogeneity and translation maturity are assessed

## Domain risks
- [ ] Reverse causation is considered for late-life risk factors
- [ ] Intervention stage, dose, comparator, adherence, and durability are considered
- [ ] Traditional/complementary interventions have reproducible composition and safety information
- [ ] Network pharmacology/docking is labeled hypothesis generating
- [ ] Caregiver and patient outcomes are not conflated
- [ ] Bibliometric patterns are not treated as biological proof

## Completion
- [ ] Allowed and prohibited claims are explicit
- [ ] Unresolved evidence and next-step study are stated
- [ ] Public communication is routed through safety auditing
- [ ] Individual medical advice is excluded
