# Scoping Review Quality Checklist

## Rationale and protocol
- [ ] Scoping review is justified over alternative review types
- [ ] Added value relative to existing reviews is stated
- [ ] Population, Concept, and Context are operational
- [ ] Primary and secondary questions are explicit
- [ ] Protocol/registration status is recorded
- [ ] Amendments and iterative changes are dated
- [ ] Team roles, funding, and conflicts are stated

## Search and selection
- [ ] Databases and grey-literature sources fit the mapping purpose
- [ ] Exact strategies, platforms, dates, and limits are preserved
- [ ] Raw exports and deduplication records are preserved
- [ ] Screening guidance was piloted
- [ ] Independent review/adjudication follows the protocol
- [ ] Full-text exclusions use one primary reason
- [ ] Counts reconcile with PRISMA-ScR flow

## Charting and mapping
- [ ] Source, study, and project IDs are linked
- [ ] Evidence types are tagged and stratified
- [ ] Charting form and data dictionary were piloted
- [ ] Author-reported, reviewer-coded, and inferred data are separated
- [ ] Missingness and abstract-only extraction are explicit
- [ ] Cross-tabulation denominators are clear
- [ ] Overlapping categories are disclosed
- [ ] Maps answer prespecified questions

## Appraisal, gaps, and consultation
- [ ] Critical appraisal decision has a rationale
- [ ] Appraisal tool matches design when used
- [ ] Influence of appraisal on synthesis is stated
- [ ] Gap categories are operational
- [ ] Low volume is not automatically called a priority
- [ ] Consultation purpose and methods are reported if conducted
- [ ] Stakeholder input is labeled separately from published evidence

## Reporting and integrity
- [ ] Conclusions do not claim effectiveness/causality without suitable methods
- [ ] Current JBI and PRISMA-ScR guidance is checked
- [ ] Protocol, searches, charting, and outputs are archived
- [ ] AI assistance and human verification are disclosed accurately
- [ ] Nothing is invented or falsely reported as completed
