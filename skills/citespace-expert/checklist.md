# CiteSpace Expert Quality Checklist

## Corpus
- [ ] Database, collection, indexes, query, timespan, and search date are recorded
- [ ] Raw exports are preserved
- [ ] Export batches are complete and nonoverlapping
- [ ] Cited references are included when required
- [ ] Record totals and file integrity are checked
- [ ] Duplicate and early-access handling is stated

## Design and parameters
- [ ] Research question matches the network type
- [ ] CiteSpace version/build is recorded
- [ ] Time slicing is justified
- [ ] Node type and link meaning are explicit
- [ ] Term sources are recorded
- [ ] Selection criterion and parameters are recorded
- [ ] Pruning method and stage are recorded
- [ ] Clustering and labeling methods are recorded
- [ ] Burst settings are recorded

## Cleaning
- [ ] Author-name variants and homonyms are assessed
- [ ] Institution aliases and parent entities are assessed
- [ ] Country/region rules are explicit
- [ ] Keyword variants and synonyms are assessed
- [ ] Raw-to-normalized mappings are reversible
- [ ] Non-equivalent concepts are not merged for convenience

## Interpretation
- [ ] Node/link counts and density are reported when relevant
- [ ] Q and silhouette are interpreted with cluster size/content
- [ ] Centrality is not equated with evidence quality
- [ ] Burst items are verified against source context
- [ ] Important clusters and records are manually inspected
- [ ] Partial years and citation lag are acknowledged

## Sensitivity and reporting
- [ ] Consequential alternative settings are compared
- [ ] Major conclusions are checked for stability
- [ ] Every figure explains encodings and parameters
- [ ] Results are bounded to the analyzed corpus
- [ ] No output or metric is invented
- [ ] Another researcher could reconstruct the analysis
